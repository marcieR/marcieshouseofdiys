The boldest thing I've ever done inside a closet (and why you should try it)

Marcie  ·  July 2024

Here's why closets are the perfect place to take design risks: the stakes are low. You're only in there for thirty seconds at a time. If you hate it, you paper over it. But if you love it — and I promise you will — it becomes the thing you show everyone who walks through your front door.

Choosing the wallpaper

I wanted something that felt like opening a door into another world. I landed on a large-scale crane print — white and bronze birds on a deep forest green background, with gold cloud details. Bold. Maximalist. Absolutely not something I'd put on a living room wall. That's exactly why it works in here.

"A closet is the perfect place to take design risks. Low stakes, high reward. It becomes the thing you show everyone who walks through your door."
On choosing bold wallpaper

Scale matters more than pattern. A large-scale print in a small space reads as intentional and dramatic. The same pattern at half the scale reads as busy. Go bigger than you think you should.

The Green paint situation

Before the wallpaper could shine, I painted the entire interior — walls, and ceiling — in a deep green. This is the step most people skip, and it's the step that makes everything else work. The paint becomes the "frame" for the wallpaper.

I used Sherwin-Williams Billiard Green. A deep green that matches the deep green in the wallpaper beautifully.

The finished closet — the brass bracket and wood hangers tie it all together.

Hardware and finishing details

The original closet rod was a wooden dowel tube on plastic brackets. I replaced it with a brass bracket hardware — a $30 change that transformed the whole thing. I also swapped the plastic shelf clips for brass ones. Nobody sees those details. I see them every morning, and that's enough.

What I used

  • Crane print paste to wall wallpaper [Milton & King]
  • Sherwin-Williams Billiard Green Semi-Gloss

Total cost: under $200. My favorite transformation per dollar of any project I've ever done.
