How to style built-in shelves so they look curated, not cluttered

Marcie  ·  April 2024

Built-in shelves are one of the most beautiful things in a room and one of the hardest to style well. Too empty and they look unfinished. Too full and they look like a storage unit. The goal is that sweet spot that reads as collected — like these things came together naturally over time.

Start with the backing

Before you put a single object on a shelf, look at the back wall. The backing is the stage. Everything else is the performance.

I use grasscloth-look peel-and-stick wallpaper inside most of my built-ins. The texture adds warmth and depth, and it makes even basic objects look more intentional in front of it.

My favorite product

NuWallpaper Grasscloth peel-and-stick from Target. Easy to apply, easy to remove, and it photographs beautifully. Cut it with a craft knife along the shelf edges for clean lines.

The rule of threes

Group objects in odd numbers. Three items, five items, seven items. Never two. Never four. The human eye finds even groupings flat and resolved. Odd groupings feel alive, like they're still in conversation with each other.

"Group objects in odd numbers. Odd groupings feel alive — like they're still in conversation with each other."

My living room built-in — note the mix of heights, the grasscloth backing, and the one unexpected object on every shelf.

Vary height, depth, and texture

  • Height: Mix tall, medium, and short items. Never two items of the same height side by side.
  • Depth: Pull some things to the shelf edge, push others back. Layering adds depth.
  • Texture: Mix matte and shiny, organic and geometric, soft and hard.

The one unexpected thing

Every shelf section needs one object that has nothing to do with "shelf decor." Something personal. Something slightly odd. Something that makes people ask about it. On my shelves: a crystal decanter from my grandmother, a $40 brass sailboat from an antique store, a ceramic horse, [something organic, like an animal or insect always feels grounding to me].

My shelf styling kit

  • NuWallpaper Grasscloth peel-and-stick (backing)
  • Small picture easel (for propping artwork)
  • Washi tape + pencil (planning layouts)
  • Small adhesive bumpers (stabilize items that tip)

Style it, live with it for a week, then adjust. Your eye will tell you what's not working.
