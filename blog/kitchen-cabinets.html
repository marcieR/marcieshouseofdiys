Budget Tips

Paint your cabinets, change your life — a complete guide to a kitchen refresh under $500

Marcie  ·  May 2024

Painting your cabinets is the single highest-impact, lowest-cost transformation in home improvement. I know people who've done it and cried actual happy tears when they saw the result. I am one of those people.

The honest prep talk

Here is the thing nobody wants to hear: prep is 80% of a good cabinet paint job. The painting takes a day. The prep takes three days. Skip the prep and you'll be back in eighteen months with peeling paint and regret.

  1. Remove all doors and hardware. Label each door with painter's tape.
  2. Clean every surface with Krud Kutter degreaser.
  3. Lightly sand with 220-grit sandpaper.
  4. Wipe down with a tack cloth.
  5. Apply one coat of bonding primer.
  6. Light sand again after primer dries.
The primer is non-negotiable

Use Zinsser BIN shellac-based bonding primer for painted or laminate cabinets. This is the reason your paint job lasts five years instead of five months.

Choosing the right paint

Use a cabinet-specific paint or hard-wearing enamel — not regular wall paint. I used Benjamin Moore Advance in Chantilly Lace. It's self-leveling (brush marks largely disappear as it dries) and cures to a hard, durable finish.

The finished kitchen — the marble contact paper on the counters was a bonus project that cost $40.

"Prep is 80% of a good cabinet paint job. Skip it and you'll be back in eighteen months with peeling paint and regret."

Application

I use a small foam roller for the flat faces of doors and a 2.5" angled brush for the recessed panels and frames. Two coats minimum, with a light sand between coats. Wait 24 hours between coats even when the paint feels dry.

Hardware: the final 10% that matters 100%

New hardware is what separates "I painted my cabinets" from "I renovated my kitchen." I replaced existing knobs with long bar pulls in brushed nickel — consistent, clean, and they make the cabinets look twice as expensive.

Full budget breakdown

  • Zinsser BIN Shellac Primer — $28
  • Benjamin Moore Advance (1 gallon) — $75
  • Foam rollers + brushes — $20
  • Sandpaper + tack cloth — $12
  • Krud Kutter degreaser — $10
  • New hardware (14 pulls) — $84
  • Total: $229
