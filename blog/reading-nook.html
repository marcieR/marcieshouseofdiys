How I built a reading nook that finally made me want to sit down

Marcie  ·  August 2024

There was a corner in my house that I had been actively ignoring for two years. It had a chair in it — a chair I never sat in — and a lamp that wasn't plugged in. Classic "I'll figure it out later" energy.

Then one day I walked past it and thought: this has so much potential. Six weeks later it became my favorite spot in the whole house.

Starting with the built-ins

The existing alcove had good bones — a natural corner with wall space on both sides. I built a simple cabinet base from MDF with panel detail on the doors, then added open shelving above that ran to the ceiling. The key to making it look custom? Crown molding at the top and column trim at the sides. Those two details do 80% of the work.

The crown molding secret

Use corner blocks (plinth blocks) at the joints instead of mitering. They look intentional, they're much easier to install, and they read as more traditional and detailed than mitered corners.

For the backing inside the shelves, I used a grasscloth-look peel-and-stick wallpaper. It adds texture and warmth without committing to full wallpapering a wall. Completely reversible. Completely charming.

The finished corner — the striped chair was the anchor piece everything else was built around.

Finding the chair first

Here's my design advice: start with the piece you love most and build the room around it. For me, that was a green-and-white striped armchair from an estate sale. The moment I saw it I knew it belonged somewhere in my house.

"Start with the piece you love most and build the room around it. Let one thing you're genuinely excited about pull everything else in."

Styling the shelves

People always ask how I style shelves without them looking cluttered. I follow three rules:

  • Rule of odds: Group objects in threes, not twos or fours.
  • Vary the height: Mix tall, medium, and small things. Never two things of identical height side by side.
  • Add one unexpected thing: A gold sailboat. A ceramic horse. Something that has nothing to do with "shelf decor" but everything to do with you.

What I used

  • MDF boards (cabinet base + shelving)
  • Panel moulding for door detail
  • Crown moulding + corner blocks
  • Column trim pieces (Home Depot)
  • Peel-and-stick grasscloth wallpaper (backing)
  • Brass cup pulls
  • Sherwin-Williams Alabaster throughout
