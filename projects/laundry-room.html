The laundry room glow-up: shiplap, open shelving, and a globe pendant that changed everything

Marcie  ·  June 2024

My laundry room used to be the room I walked through as fast as possible. Beige walls, fluorescent light, wire shelving. It felt like the back room of a storage unit.

You spend real time in your laundry room. You start your day there. You end your day there. It deserves to feel good.

The shiplap wall

I started with the walls. I added horizontal shiplap boards on the back wall — a two-day project that completely transformed the bones of the room.

I used 1×6 pine boards with a nickel gap between each row. Primed and painted them Sherwin-Williams Alabaster before installing — much easier than cutting in around boards already on the wall.

Shiplap tip

Use a nickel as your spacing guide between boards. Slide it along as you nail each board in place. Every gap will be perfectly consistent.

Open shelving over the machines

I replaced the wire shelving with a simple floating shelf — a single 1×10 pine board on heavy-duty hidden brackets. One shelf. Styled with a glass jar for detergent pods, a woven basket, a small plant, a framed print. It feels like a real room.

The finished laundry room — the sage green cabinet paint was the last addition and it pulled everything together.

The globe pendant (the whole point)

The fluorescent light was a $12 builder-grade rectangle. I replaced it with a clear globe pendant on a brass socket for $45. It makes me happy every single time the light is on.

"The light fixture is the jewelry of a room. Change it. Even in the laundry room. Especially in the laundry room."

The sage green cabinets

I painted the upper cabinet doors in Sherwin-Williams Softened Green — a muted sage that reads almost like a neutral. This was the last thing I did and the detail that tied the whole room together.

What I used

  • 1×6 pine boards (shiplap)
  • Sherwin-Williams Alabaster (walls + shiplap)
  • Sherwin-Williams Softened Green (cabinet doors)
  • Globe pendant light + brass socket (~$45)
  • 1×10 pine floating shelf + hidden brackets
  • Glass storage jars + woven basket
